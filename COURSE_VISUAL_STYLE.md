# Course Visual Style Guide

## Style name
Readable Stylised Course Polish

## Core principles
- Gameplay readability comes first.
- Surface boundaries stay clear even with added texture.
- Course detail should feel rich, stylised, and mobile friendly.
- Repeated props should come from a reusable prop library.
- Surface shapes should be organic and rounded, not perfect ellipses.

## Surface treatment rules
### Fairway
- Clear colour separation from rough.
- Subtle striped mow pattern.
- Gentle tonal variation.
- Natural outline with rounded bends.

### Rough
- Darker, patchier grass texture.
- Slight noise and tufting.
- Softer, more organic edges.

### Green
- Smooth short-cut texture.
- Distinct collar/fringe around the green.
- Readable but subtle slope visualisation in putting view.
- Organic rounded shape, not a perfect oval.

### Bunkers
- Rounded natural shape.
- Rake texture.
- Lip shadow to suggest depth.
- Clean readable edge against surrounding grass.

### Water
- Natural shoreline shape with soft bends.
- Darker shoreline edge.
- Very subtle animated shimmer / ripple texture.
- Animation should support atmosphere and not distract from gameplay.

## Reusable prop system
Props should be defined once and reused by type across all holes. Each future hole places instances of reusable props rather than inventing new visuals ad hoc.

### Required prop families
- Small tee sign
- Tee markers
- Yardage markers
- Bench
- Ball washer post
- Tree variants
- Shrub variants
- Rock variants

### Optional prop families for future holes
These may not appear on every hole but should belong to the same design language.
- Bridge variants
- Cart path markers
- Rope/fence posts
- Stone walls
- Retaining edges
- Reeds and water-edge plants
- Flower beds
- Distance boards
- Directional posts

## Placement rules
- Keep the main line of play relatively uncluttered.
- Put most decorative detail around the tee, outside fairway corridors, around water, beside greens, and in out-of-play framing zones.
- Trees and shrubs should frame the hole but not hide the ball.
- Props should support the fantasy of a golf course while preserving readability.

## Future hole notes
Each hole should define:
- Natural fairway shape
- Natural green shape
- Natural bunker and water shapes
- Surface textures
- Prop instances referencing the shared prop library
- Optional signature dressing such as bridges or walls where appropriate
